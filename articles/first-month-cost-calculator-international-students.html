Move-in Budget

First month abroad cost calculator for international students

The first month abroad is rarely a normal month. Students often budget for rent and food, then get surprised by deposits, temporary housing, bedding, cookware, phone setup, airport transport, and timing gaps before a lease or school plan starts.

Use the calculator first

Open the First Month Abroad Calculator, choose a city, set your housing plan, add deposit months, temporary housing nights, and travel cost. Keep the result separate from tuition and your normal monthly budget.

What the first-month estimate includes

  • Security deposit and upfront rent.
  • Temporary housing before your lease or dorm begins.
  • One-way flight, airport transfer, or move-in travel.
  • Bedding, kitchen basics, cleaning supplies, adapters, and small home items.
  • Phone plan, transit card, local setup costs, and emergency buffer.

Why this is useful for SEO and sharing

Students and parents search for a concrete number when planning a move. A city living-cost article is useful, but a first-month calculator gives them a result they can copy, screenshot, and discuss with family before signing a lease.

FAQ and internal links

Should tuition be included in the first-month calculator?

No. Keep tuition separate unless you personally need to treat tuition as monthly cash flow. This tool is for move-in cash before the first normal month starts.
