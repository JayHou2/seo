Chicago student cost 2026: UChicago monthly budget guide

Chicago can be cheaper than coastal college cities, but students still need to budget carefully. For UChicago students, housing in or near Hyde Park, winter preparation, transit habits, and school-billed costs all shape the real monthly number.

Quick monthly estimate

A shared-housing Chicago budget often starts around $1,900 to $2,900 per month before tuition. A private apartment, frequent dining out, or extra rideshare costs can move the monthly budget higher.

Budget categories

  • Housing: compare residence halls, shared apartments, private studios, and lease length.
  • Food: meal plan, groceries, cafes, restaurants, and delivery.
  • Transit: CTA, campus shuttles, rideshare, airport trips, and late-night travel.
  • Winter setup: coat, boots, bedding, and heating-related utility costs if off campus.
  • Insurance and supplies: health insurance, renters insurance, books, software, and equipment.

Why first month matters

The first month in Chicago can cost much more than a normal month because of deposits, temporary housing, bedding, kitchen setup, winter clothing, and airport transportation. Build a separate move-in budget before comparing monthly averages.

Is Chicago a good budget city for students?

It can be, especially compared with New York, Los Angeles, and the Bay Area. But the final answer depends on housing, commute, winter setup, and whether school-billed costs are included.
