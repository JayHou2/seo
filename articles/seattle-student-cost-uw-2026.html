Seattle student cost 2026: UW monthly budget guide

Seattle can feel more manageable than New York or the Bay Area, but University of Washington students still need to budget carefully. Housing near campus, winter setup, transit, and first-month cash can surprise new students.

Quick monthly estimate

A shared-housing UW Seattle budget often starts around $2,000 to $3,000 per month before tuition. A private apartment, car costs, frequent dining out, or short-term housing can move the budget higher.

What to include

  • Rent and utilities: compare University District, shared apartments, and farther transit-connected areas.
  • Food: groceries, campus dining, restaurants, coffee, and delivery.
  • Transit: bus, light rail, rideshare, bike, and airport trips.
  • Insurance: health insurance, renters insurance, and any plan required by lease or school.
  • First-month setup: deposit, temporary housing, bedding, kitchen supplies, rain gear, and winter clothing.

How to decide

Run Seattle in the StudyCost calculator, then compare that monthly number with UW's annual student budget. If the annual budget looks reasonable but the move-in month looks tight, add more cash buffer before signing a lease.
