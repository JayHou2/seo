UCLA cost of living 2026: what should students budget each month?

UCLA students often compare the official annual cost of attendance with the much more practical question: how much cash do I need each month in Los Angeles? The answer depends heavily on housing near Westwood, whether you share a room or apartment, and whether you need a car.

Quick monthly estimate

For a shared-housing plan, a practical UCLA monthly living-cost range is about $2,300 to $3,300 before tuition. A private studio, car costs, or frequent rideshare and food delivery can move the budget higher.

Budget categories to separate

  • Rent: Westwood convenience is valuable, but shared housing usually matters more than small grocery savings.
  • Food: meal plan, groceries, campus dining, coffee, and delivery should be counted separately.
  • Transit and car costs: Los Angeles can be manageable without a car in some student routines, but parking, insurance, gas, and rideshare can change the budget.
  • Health insurance: confirm whether school insurance is included in your bill before buying another plan.
  • First-month setup: deposit, temporary housing, bedding, kitchen items, and move-in transport often arrive before the first normal month.

How to use this page

Start with the StudyCost calculator, then compare your estimate with UCLA and UC official cost pages. If the number seems too low, check whether you forgot insurance, books, local transportation, or deposits.

Is Westwood worth the higher rent?

It can be worth it if it saves enough commute time and stress. But students should compare the total cost, not only rent: transport, parking, lease terms, and roommate setup all matter.
