Budget Template

International student monthly budget template

A good student budget should answer one question: how much cash do I need each month after I arrive, separate from tuition? This template gives you the categories to fill before accepting an offer or signing a lease.

Monthly budget categories

  • Housing: rent, utilities, internet, laundry, and renter setup costs.
  • Food: groceries, campus meals, restaurants, coffee, and delivery fees.
  • Transit: subway, bus, rail, bike share, rideshare, and airport trips.
  • Insurance: school health plan, private health cover, renters insurance, or travel insurance.
  • Phone and banking: SIM or eSIM, account fees, ATM fees, and wire transfer costs.
  • Academic supplies: books, software, lab materials, printing, and equipment.
  • Personal spending: clothing, gym, events, short trips, and small emergencies.

First-month budget categories

The first month is rarely normal. Add deposits, temporary housing, bedding, cookware, adapters, cleaning supplies, winter clothing, and local ID or transport setup. If your rent is due before financial aid or family transfers arrive, cash timing matters as much as total cost.

FAQ and internal links

Should I include tuition in my monthly budget?

Include it only if you personally need to spread tuition into monthly cash flow. Otherwise, keep tuition separate so living costs stay clear.
