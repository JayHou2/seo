Insurance

Do students need renters insurance in the US?

Renters insurance is easy to ignore until a lease, landlord, or parent asks about it. For students, the answer depends on whether you live in a dorm, an off-campus apartment, or a shared rental.

When students should pay attention

  • You are signing an off-campus lease in your own name.
  • Your landlord or building asks for proof of renters insurance.
  • You own a laptop, phone, camera, bike, instruments, or other expensive items.
  • You want liability coverage if someone is injured in your rented space.
  • You are not sure whether a parent or family policy extends to your housing situation.

Dorms and family policies

Some dorm students may have limited coverage through a parent or family policy, but limits vary. Off-campus students are more likely to need their own policy. The safest move is to ask the insurer directly and check the lease requirements before move-in.

Budget impact

Renters insurance is usually a smaller line item than rent or food, but it belongs in the monthly budget because it can be required before keys are released. Add it next to phone, transit, and health insurance in your StudyCost estimate.

Is renters insurance the same as health insurance?

No. Renters insurance relates to personal property, liability, and covered housing disruptions. Health insurance relates to medical care.
