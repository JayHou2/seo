USC Los Angeles student cost 2026: monthly budget guide

USC students need to think about Los Angeles living costs as a cash-flow problem. Tuition is only one part of the bill. Rent, food, transport, health insurance, and first-month move-in costs can decide whether a plan feels comfortable or stressful.

Quick monthly estimate

A shared-apartment budget for a USC student often starts around $2,400 to $3,400 per month before tuition. Private housing, car ownership, paid parking, or frequent food delivery can move the number higher.

What to compare before choosing housing

  • Distance to campus and late-night safety.
  • Lease length, security deposit, and whether furniture is included.
  • Roommate count, private bedroom cost, and shared utility rules.
  • Transit, bike, rideshare, parking, and car insurance.
  • Food plan versus groceries and off-campus meals.

First-month cash matters

Many students can afford a normal month but get surprised by move-in timing. Add deposits, application fees, temporary stays, bedding, cookware, phone setup, and transport from the airport before deciding how much cash to bring.

Should USC students live closest to campus?

Close housing can save time and reduce late-night transport stress, but it is not automatically cheaper. Compare total monthly cost, commute pattern, and lease conditions.
